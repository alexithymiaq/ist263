Introduction

Pokémon cards are rare for many reasons. Some are limited print runs, tournament exclusives, errors/misprints, or even just a high likeability from fans. This section will showcase some of the most iconic cards with background information, their rarity, and sales history!

Iconic Cards

Base Set Charizard (1999)

  • First edition PSA 10 version has sold for over $400,000
  • Symbol of the 1999 Pokémon Craze
  • Fun fact: Known as the “Holy Grail” for many collectors

Pikachu Illustrator (1998)

  • Only around 40 known copies exist
  • Has reached sales of over $6 million at auction
  • Fun fact: Awarded to winners of a Japanese illustration contest

Shining Charizard (Neo Destiny, 2002)

  • Popular for introducing alternate color Pokémon cards
  • Fun fact: One of the first “Shining” cards with different holofoil art

Trophy Cards

  • Some exist in quantities as low as fewer than 10
  • Fun fact: These cards were awarded only at Japanese tournaments

Modern Icons

  • Flying Pikachu; promotional card featuring Pikachu with wings
  • Base Set Blastoise and Venusaur; The other two original starter Pokémon cards from the base set

Why These Cards Are Special

You may be wondering why are these cards so special? Theres a multitude of reasons for each, the cards listed above only cover a small amount of the rare and iconic cards. Most iconic cards are of limited supply and high in rarity. They can also hold high historical value, marking milestones in TCG history; look at the base set cards for Charizard, Blastoise, and Venusaur! High sales prices show that these cards are still high in demand among collectors.

Often though, cards hold high emotional value to players. Even amongst non-hardcore Pokémon fans, collecting a card of your favorite Pokémon can raise its sentiment value. Even within the community, Pokémon cards can symbolize cultural impact amongst game releases, movies, and updates to TCG.
