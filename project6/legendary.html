Introduction

Pokémon cards are rare for many reasons. Some are limited print runs, tournament exclusives, errors/misprints, or even just a high likeability from fans. This section will showcase some of the most iconic cards with background information, their rarity, and sales history!

Iconic Cards

Base Set Charizard (1999)

  • First edition PSA 10 version has sold for over $400,000
  • Symbol of the 1999 Pokémon Craze
  • Fun fact: Known as the “Holy Grail” for many collectors

Pikachu Illustrator (1998)

  • Only around 40 known copies exist
  • Has reached sales of over $6 million at auction
  • Fun fact: Awarded to winners of a Japanese illustration contest

Shining Charizard (2002)

  • Introduced alternate color Pokémon
  • One of the first “Shining” cards

Trophy Cards

  • Some exist in quantities less than 10
  • Awarded only at Japanese tournaments

Modern Icons

  • Flying Pikachu promo
  • Base Set Blastoise & Venusaur

Why These Cards Are So Special

You may be wondering why these cards are so special. There are a multitude of reasons for each. Most iconic cards are scarce with limited print runs or high rarity. They also hold historical value, marking milestones in TCG history—like the base set Charizard, Blastoise, and Venusaur.

Cards also hold emotional value to players. Collecting your favorite Pokémon makes cards culturally meaningful and sentimental within the community.
