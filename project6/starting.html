Collecting for Beginners

Where to Start

You can start by buying themed decks or preconstructed sets to get a feel for the game. I reccomend starting with affordable, modern sets to avoid scams and fake cards.

Some of these sets include:

  • Sword & Shield Base Set
  • Scarlet & Violet Base Set
  • Pokemon go Set

Tips for Collectors

  • Keep your cards in protective sleeves and binders
  • Learn about card conditions (Near Mint, PSA grading, etc)
  • Trade Fairly; know card values though TCGPlayer

Building a Beginner Deck

Basics of Deck Building

There are 60 cards in total in a deck. They are divded up as 20 Pokemon, 20 trainers, and 20 energies. Although this can vary as you play and build your own strategies! You should choose a type (Fire, Grass, Fairy, Psychic, and more!) and build around it!

Common Beginner Deck Types

Theme Decks: Pre-made balanced decks. These are good for starting out and getting a feel for the game!

Competitive Starter Decks: Also pre-made but modified to focus on certain strategies. these are reccomended once you get a feel for the game but also want to focus on specifics (defense, offense, etc.)

Popular Sets for Deck Building (U.S & Japan)

Some famous sets include:

  • Evolving Skies (famous for Eeveelutions)
  • Champion's Path (Charizard Chase)
  • VSTAR Universe (Japanese Set with beautiful alt arts)

How To Play The Game (Basics)

Objective

Knock out your opponent’s Pokémon and collect all prize cards (or whatever else is at stake)

Setup

Each player has a deck of 60. You'll then draw 7 cards, place 1 active Pokemon, and set up 5 on your bench.

Turn Structure

Each player will draw a card, play a basic Pokemon, evolve Pokemon, or attach energy. You can play trainer cards for effects and attack if you have the required energy.

Winning Conditions

The goal is to collect all prize cards, ensure your opponent cannot draw a card at the start of their turn, or if your opponent has no Pokemon left! You can click this link to watch a youtube video overview on how to play!

Trading Tips

  • Trade based on card values and not just looks (While this is also okay, but for less competitive players)
  • Learn how to spot fake cards (blurry prints, wrong fonts, fake holofoil, etc)
  • Build trust with trading partners, especially when trading online
