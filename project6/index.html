About Me

My name is Alejandra. I am a current Senior at Syracuse University, and I have been collecting Pokémon cards since I was 8 years old.

My History

I first got into Pokémon when I was around 6 or 7 years old. My older cousins were huge fans and my journey started with watching Saturday morning episodes of the Pokémon anime. A world filled with mythical creatures that could be your companions fascinated me, and soon my cousins introduced me to the Pokémon TCG.

I would beg my parents for booster packs and carefully fill binders with my growing collection. My first themed set starred my favorite Pokémon at the time—Jigglypuff! Though I lost many playground battles, I learned and improved, and Pokémon became a way to connect with friends and family. This website celebrates that love and history.

Pokemon TCG History & Overview

Origins (1996–1999)

Pokémon TCG was first published in Japan by Media Factory in 1996, then brought to the U.S. by Wizards of the Coast in 1999. The Base Set featured iconic cards like “Base Set Charizard.”

Growth & Evolution

Pokémon exploded in popularity during the 1999–2000 craze, expanding into multiple sets, tournaments, and becoming a cultural phenomenon in trading card history.

Competitive Play

Pokémon TCG isn’t just about collecting — it features official tournaments and a competitive world championship that has continued strong since the early 2000s.

Why Fans Collect & Play

  • Nostalgia: Many players grew up with Pokémon and cherish the cards.
  • Artwork: Each card features unique illustrations by different artists.
  • Value: Some rare cards are highly sought after and valuable.
  • Community: Trading and playing create friendships worldwide.
