About Me

My name is Alejandra. I am a current Senior at Syracuse University, and I have been collecting Pokémon cards since I was 8 years old.

My History

I first got into Pokémon when I was around 6 or 7 years old. My older cousins were huge fans and my journey started with waking up on Saturday mornings to sit in the living room and watch new episodes. A world filled with mythical creatures that could be your companion just fascinated me and soon my older cousins introduced me into TCG. From there I would beg my mother or my father at the store for a new pack or to go to stores and see what I could find. I was obsessed to say the least. My father bought me binder after binder as my collection grew. I kept duplicates until there were pages dedicated to one Pokémon. Eventually in middle school I created my first ever actual set, starring my favorite Pokémon at the time, Jigglypuff. Of course I lost many battles, but nevertheless I continued to pursue victory amongst the boys in my class. I would rush to finish my lunch so I could pull out my cards and challenge the boys outside on the pavement. Over time I learned and made better sets, I showed off cards I had that other students did not. I am 20 now, and since then my love for Pokémon has not died. After moving around as a child, I unfortunately lost my binders, but I remain in the world of Pokémon through games and rewatching the tv shows. This website is made with love and dedication. For me, Pokémon was never just a card game or a tv show, it was love shared between my cousins and I showing off which cards we pulled, between my father and I sitting in the living room with a bowl of cereal watching the show. This website is a page dedicated to that love and to highlight the amazing history and stories of Pokémon TCG.

Pokemon TCG History & Overview

Origins (1996-1999)

Pokémon TCG was first published in Japan by Media Factory in 1996, then brought to the U.S. by Wizards of the Coast in 1999.
The first expansion was the Base Set featuring the legendary "Base Set Charizard"

Growth & Evolution

Pokemon exploded in populatiry during the 1999-2000 Pokemon Craze. It ended up expanding into multiple sets, tournaments, and became a staple in trading card culture.

Competitive Play

Pokemon TCG isn't just about collecting; it has offical tournaments and a competitive world championship. Organized play began in the early 2000's and is still going strong today!

Why Fans Collect & Play

  • Nostalgia: Many players grew up with Pokémon and still cherish the cards, I know I do!
  • Artwork: Cards feature unique illustrations by many different artists. These cards are framed and even sold just for the artwork or to tell a story
  • Value: Some rare cards sell for thousands of dollars
  • Community: Playing and trading creates bonds between fans worldwide.
