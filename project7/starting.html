Collecting for Beginners

Where to Start

You can start by buying themed decks or preconstructed sets, which are designed to be beginner-friendly and give you a solid introduction to how the game works. These products come with balanced cards and clear strategies, making it easier to learn the rules, practice gameplay, and understand different mechanics without feeling overwhelmed. I recommend starting with affordable, modern sets, since they are widely available, easier to verify as authentic, and less likely to involve scams or fake cards. Once you’re more comfortable, you can gradually expand your collection with booster packs or singles that fit your play style and goals.

Tips for Collectors

  • Keep cards in protective sleeves and binders
  • Learn card conditions (Near Mint, PSA grading)
  • Know card values using TCGPlayer

Building a Beginner Deck

Basics of Deck Building

A standard deck consists of 60 cards total, typically divided into 20 Pokémon, 20 Trainer cards, and 20 Energy cards. This balance helps new players learn the flow of the game and understand how each card type works together. As you gain more experience, this ratio can change depending on your strategy, play style, and the deck you are building. It’s also important to choose a specific Pokémon type—such as Fire, Grass, Fairy, Psychic, and many more. Build your deck around that type to create stronger synergy and more consistent gameplay.

Common Beginner Deck Types

Theme Decks are pre-made, well-balanced decks designed for beginners. They are great for starting out, learning the rules, and getting comfortable with how a deck should function during a match.

Competitive Starter Decks are also pre-made, but they are more refined and focused on specific strategies. These decks are recommended once you understand the basics and want to improve your gameplay by emphasizing certain strengths, such as strong offense, solid defense, or efficient energy use.

How To Play & Trading Tips

How To Play The Game

Objective

Knock out your opponent’s Pokémon and collect all prize cards.

Setup

Draw 7 cards, place 1 Active Pokémon, and set up 5 on the bench.

Turn Structure

Draw, play Pokémon, attach energy, use Trainer cards, and attack.

Winning Conditions

Take all prize cards, leave your opponent with no Pokémon, or deck them out.

Trading Tips

  • Trade based on card value, not just appearance
  • Learn to spot fake cards (fonts, holofoil, print quality)
  • Build trust when trading online or in person
