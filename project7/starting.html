Collecting for Beginners

Where to Start

You can start by buying themed decks or preconstructed sets to get a feel for the game. I recommend starting with affordable, modern sets to avoid scams and fake cards.

Tips for Collectors

  • Keep cards in protective sleeves and binders
  • Learn card conditions (Near Mint, PSA grading)
  • Know card values using TCGPlayer

Building a Beginner Deck

Basics of Deck Building

There are 60 cards in total in a deck. They are divded up as 20 Pokemon, 20 trainers, and 20 energies. Although this can vary as you play and build your own strategies! You should choose a type (Fire, Grass, Fairy, Psychic, and more!) and build around it!

Common Beginner Deck Types

Theme Decks: Pre-made balanced decks. These are good for starting out and getting a feel for the game!

Competitive Starter Decks: Also pre-made but modified to focus on certain strategies. these are reccomended once you get a feel for the game but also want to focus on specifics (defense, offense, etc.)

How To Play The Game (Basics)

Objective

Knock out your opponent’s Pokémon and collect all prize cards (or meet other win conditions).

Setup

Each player draws 7 cards, places 1 Active Pokémon, and sets up 5 Pokémon on the bench.

Turn Structure

Draw a card, play Pokémon, attach energy, use Trainer cards, and attack if possible.

Winning Conditions

Win by taking all prize cards, leaving your opponent with no Pokémon, or preventing them from drawing a card.
